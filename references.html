REFERENCES

Bale Opener Machine

Prototype Bale Opener Machine has been conceptually designed with reference to the industrial Unifloc A 12 Bale Opener by Rieter. The Unifloc A 12 is known for its ability to supply cards reliably with 2400 kg/h production lines. It incorporates a patented force measurement system for scanning the bale laydown, ensuring high productivity from the very start — even when working with new bale groups.

Machine Specifications

Metal Detector Machine

The conceptual design and structural layout of this Prototype Metal Detector Machine has been developed with reference to the industrial-grade Metal and Spark Detection System by Rieter. This system is renowned for its ability to reliably detect and remove both magnetic and non-magnetic metallic particles from cotton and synthetic fiber tufts in high-capacity blowroom lines. The Rieter Metal and Spark Detector is engineered for production rates of up to 2 400 kg/h, seamlessly integrating into blowroom processes due to its compact, space-saving design.

Machine Specifications

• Width: 800 mm to 1500 mm
• Height: 1000 mm to 2000 mm
• Length: 1200 mm to 3000 mm
• Conveyor Width: 600 mm to 1200 mm
• Detection Tunnel Size: 600 mm × 800 mm to 1000 mm × 1500 mm (adjustable based on material flow)

Pre-cleaner Machine

The Prototype Pre-cleaner Machine has been conceptually designed with reference to the industrial Rieter B-15 Precleaner system. The B-15 is recognized for its ability to efficiently remove impurities from fiber tufts while preserving fiber quality — achieving production line capacities of up to 2400 kg/h. The system features an optimized grid bar cross-section, ideal material routing, and a large dedusting surface to maintain high cleaning efficiency, reaching up to 90%.

Machine Specifications

• Working Width: 1600 mm
• Machine Length: 2227 mm
• Machine Width: 1040 mm
• Machine Height: 2020 mm
• Cleaning Roller Diameter: 750 mm

Multimixer Machine

The Prototype Multimixer Machine has been conceptually designed with reference to the industrial Trutzschler CL-C3 Multimixer system. The CL-C3 is recognized for its critical role in controlled mixing operations, ensuring a homogeneous blend of fiber tufts before the final cleaning stage in blowroom lines. This system is specially engineered to process severely contaminated cottons and mixed fiber types in short, compact lines. In combination with pre-cleaners like the CL-P, it offers the flexibility to handle almost all cotton grades — providing spinning mills with future-ready reliability and long-term investment protection. The core function of the multimixer is to open, mix, and evenly blend fiber tufts while maintaining controlled feeding to subsequent machines. Inside, three rotating beaters fitted with carding saw tooth wire operate at progressively higher speeds (typically 900, 1100, and 1400 rpm).

Machine Specifications

  • Frame Width: 1600 mm
  • Machine Length: 2455 mm
  • Machine Height: 1250 mm

fine-Cleaner Machine

The Prototype Fine Cleaner Machine has been conceptually designed with reference to the industrial TS-T3 Fine Cleaner system. The TS-T3 is renowned for its ability to perform highly efficient final cleaning of cotton tufts before they enter the carding stage, ensuring the removal of even the smallest seed coat fragments, micro-dust particles, and remaining contaminants — delivering consistently clean raw material for high-quality yarn production. This fine cleaning system typically operates as the last cleaning process in the blowroom line. It is engineered to deliver superior fiber protection while maintaining high cleaning efficiency and throughput capacity. The cleaning zone comprises a combination of high-speed beaters with precision saw tooth wire and multi-stage grid bar assemblies, optimized to achieve deep cleaning without compromising fiber length or quality.

Machine Specifications

  • Length: 2618 mm
  • Width: 1200 mm
  • Height: 4450 mm

Gratitude

We would like to extend our heartfelt gratitude to the renowned textile machinery manufacturers Trützschler and Rieter for their invaluable contributions to the field of textile engineering. The technical brochures, operational manuals, and machine specifications made publicly available by these industry leaders played a crucial role in the conceptualization and development of our project, “Development of a Laboratory Scale Miniaturized Blowroom Prototype Model.” The detailed insights provided through their product literature and system overviews of components such as the Bale Opener, Metal Detector, Pre-cleaner, Multimixer, and Fine-cleaner helped us understand the principles and mechanisms behind full-scale industrial equipment. This knowledge enabled us to translate complex operations into a compact, functional, and educational prototype model suitable for laboratory use. We deeply appreciate the openness with which Trützschler and Rieter share technical knowledge, and we acknowledge their role in empowering students, educators, and researchers alike to bridge the gap between theoretical learning and practical application. Their contributions have significantly enriched the academic value and technical integrity of our project. Thank you for being a continuous source of inspiration and innovation in the world of textile manufacturing.
