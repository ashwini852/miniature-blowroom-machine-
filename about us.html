About Us

We are a dedicated group of final-year B.Tech students (Batch 2024‚Äì2025) from the Department of Textile Technology at Shri Guru Gobind Singhji Institute of Engineering and Technology, Vishnupuri, Nanded. Our project, titled "Development of a Laboratory Scale Miniaturized Blowroom Prototype Model", is the result of our collective efforts to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and hands-on experience in the field of textile engineering. Recognizing the need for practical exposure in fiber preparation and processing, we undertook the challenge of designing and building a fully functional blowroom prototype that could be used at the laboratory level for educational and research purposes...

The blowroom is the first and one of the most crucial stages in the textile spinning process, responsible for opening, cleaning, and blending raw fibers such as cotton and synthetics. Our miniature model replicates this process on a small scale using simplified yet functional versions of industrial machinery. The prototype consists of a Bale Opener, Metal Detector, Pre-cleaner, Multimixer, and Fine-cleaner, each built using lightweight materials like PVC pipes, foam sheets, acrylic panels, and fitted with BLDC motors for motion control. This setup not only demonstrates the actual working of blowroom machinery but also helps in understanding the sequence and technical principles behind fiber processing.

The main objective of developing this model was to create a cost-effective, compact, and accessible tool for students and researchers. It allows them to visualize and interact with the process of fiber opening and cleaning in a controlled environment, making learning more immersive and practical. The prototype also serves as a platform to test new materials and configurations without the need for full-scale industrial equipment, thus promoting innovation and experimentation.

Our project was developed under the valuable guidance of Dr. Prakash A. Khude, Head of the Department, whose mentorship and technical insight played a crucial role in shaping our work. The team behind this project includes Omkar Bhosale, Tejas Bachhav, Ashwini Shinde, Chaitali Turankar, Sakshi Suroshe, Kishor Panhalkar, Dhannasingh Dakhane, Aniket Rahangdale, and Gaurav Ghorband. Together, we collaborated on the design, fabrication, and documentation of the model, ensuring that each component met both functional and educational goals.

Through this project, we aim to contribute a useful teaching aid for textile engineering labs and inspire future students to explore the practical side of textile manufacturing technology. We believe that combining innovation with education is the key to building a more skilled and industry-ready generation of textile engineers.
